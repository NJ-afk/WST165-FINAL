My Haworthia Collection

These are my personal collection of Haworthias. I find it fascinating how they come in different forms and patterns. I especially love how their colors change when slightly sun-stressed. I personally took these photos one sunny afternoon, and it felt peaceful and satisfying capturing their beauty.

Eagle's Claw

Eagle’s Claw Succulent (Haworthiopsis reinwardtii) A compact succulent with upright, stacked green leaves covered in raised white spots. Its textured, clustered growth makes it a striking choice for indoor pots and succulent collections.

Haworthia Glauca Var Herrei

Haworthia Glauca Var Herrei A compact succulent with blue-green, frosted leaves arranged in tight rosettes. Native to South Africa, it is drought-tolerant, low-maintenance, and safe for pets.

Zebra Plant

Zebra Plant (Haworthia fasciata) A slow-growing succulent with bold white stripes and a rosette shape. A long-lasting and popular indoor plant.

Haworthia ‘Assid Starfish’

A star-shaped succulent with textured leaves that can turn orange under bright light.

Haworthia Retusa

A compact succulent with translucent “windowed” leaves that absorb light beautifully.

Aloe–Haworthia Hybrid

Also known as Hankey Dwarf Aloe. A textured, low-maintenance succulent perfect for beginners.
